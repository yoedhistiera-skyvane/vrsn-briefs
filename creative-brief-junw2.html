Meta Ad Policy | Safe But Strong
Mandatory. Compare batch was 100% flagged
Do NOT use
  • Competitor brand names or logos (Kuru, Hoka, Orthofeet, any real brand). This killed the entire Compare batch
  • Fabricated scores, rankings, "we tested 4," "Editor's Choice," star-rating charts
  • Invented stats or percentages not substantiated by the brand
  • "ENDS / CURES / FIXES / TREATS" a condition in AD copy. "Foot Pain Ends Here" is the LP hero, it is NOT a Meta-safe ad headline. In ads, soften to comfort and relief observations
  • Personal-attribute call-outs: "your plantar fasciitis," "do your feet hurt," "are you in pain"
  • Person before/after implying a health transformation
  • Fake health-assessment hooks ("Free Foot Health Analysis")
DO use
  • Observational, universal framing: "the ache that shows up by 4pm," not "your foot pain"
  • Product truth: wide toe box, knit upper, foam outsole, no break-in, 4 colorways
  • Real substantiated proof: 100-day money-back guarantee, No Break-In Ever, Podiatrist Approved (confirm substantiation)
  • Product-only comparison: narrow tapered shoe vs the vrsn shape, never a named brand
  • Real, verifiable customer testimonials with star emoji
  • The "instant comfort + long-term foot health" balance story
This is health-adjacent advertising. The brand line "FOOT PAIN ENDS HERE" lives on the landing page where it belongs. Ad-side, every line must read as comfort, fit, or all-day-wear observation, never a medical promise. If a headline could be read as "this product treats a condition," rewrite it before it ships.

Ad-to-LP Symphony | the conversion contract
Ads → LP → Add to Cart → Checkout → Buy
Read before writing a single headline

A click is a promise. The LP must keep that promise in the first screen, or the click dies and CPA rises. Last period proved it: PF had the best CTR (5.3%) and weak ROAS (1.17) because the ad promise and the page experience did not match. Every asset in this batch follows the contract below.

Three rules that protect conversion: (1) The ad may not promise anything the LP does not deliver above the fold; if the LP cannot pay it off, change the ad, not the promise. (2) Tone continuity: dark-surface ads land best on story LPs that open dark; match the visual temperature so the click does not feel like a site-switch. (3) The CTA verb on the ad ("Read the story") must match what actually happens (an advertorial), never "Shop now" into an article. Intent built in the ad must be spent on the page, not reset.

Standing Instruction for Blitzads

This batch is about doing more of what worked. Section 1 is the priority. Clone the proven winners and change one variable at a time. The single most important fix is diversifying Travel, currently carried by one creative (0025).

The ad and the LP are one product. Every asset promises only what its landing page pays off above the fold, matches its visual temperature, and uses a CTA verb that tells the truth about what the click opens. Intent built in the ad must be spent on the page, not reset. The funnel is Ads → LP → Add to Cart → Checkout → Buy, and the asset is judged on the whole chain, not the click.

Brand system is now official: Silka Bold caps headlines, Figtree body, #1D1D1D / #212121 surfaces, #B8DE55 accent for the good, #F36D4E for the pain, PRO/CON badges, and [vrsn] always with brackets. Never VRSN, never Vrsn, never vrsn without brackets in the logo lockup.

Product accuracy is non-negotiable. Knit upper, 4 eyelets, wide toe box, correct colorways, [vrsn] embossed on the medial sole in the sole tone. Last batch we flagged 5-eyelet renders, canvas looks, wrong colorways, parenthesis and red logos, and leaked prompt text. If it does not look real, regenerate.

Meta safety holds throughout. No competitor names or logos, no fabricated scores, no invented stats, no personal-attribute health call-outs, no cure language in ads ("FOOT PAIN ENDS HERE" stays on the LP), no price. Lean on the real proof: 100-day guarantee, No Break-In Ever, Podiatrist Approved if substantiated.
